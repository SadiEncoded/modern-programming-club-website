## **Article 5: Membership**

### **5.1 Eligibility**

Membership in the DIC Programming Club (DICPC) shall be open to all enrolled students of Daffodil International College who are passionate about programming, technology, and learning, and who seek to further the objectives of the Club.

### **5.2 Types of Members**

All members admitted to the Club shall be bound by this Constitution and the By-laws of the Club, shall be liable for any fees and subscriptions prescribed, and shall be entitled to all rights and privileges associated with membership.

1. **Ordinary Member**: Full-time DIC students actively participating in Club activities. Financial members are eligible to hold any office and enjoy all privileges of membership. The majority of Club members shall be enrolled students of DIC.

2. **Associate Member (Affiliate Member)**: Alumni or part-time students of DIC who are actively involved in Club events. Financial members in this category may hold office and enjoy privileges of membership, subject to DICSP regulations regarding non-full-time students in executive roles.

3. **Social Member**: Individuals interested in supporting and promoting the Club, but who do not wish to participate in core objectives. Social Members do not hold executive positions and are typically ineligible to vote in formal Club elections.

### **5.3 Rights and Responsibilities**

All members shall:

1. Respect and abide by this Constitution, the directives of the Faculty Advisor, and decisions of the Executive Committee.

2. Maintain a minimum GPA of 2.50 unless a specific exemption is granted by the Executive Committee in consultation with the Faculty Advisor (DICSP Article‚ÄØ7.3).

3. Actively participate in Club activities, events, and initiatives to support the Club‚Äôs objectives.

4. Adhere to the Code of Conduct (Article‚ÄØ12) and contribute to upholding the Club‚Äôs core values.

5. Exercise voting rights in Club elections and major decisions (Article‚ÄØ10).

6. Propose ideas and initiatives aligned with the Club‚Äôs aims.

7. Participate in meetings, events, and leadership opportunities appropriate to their membership type.

8. Access non-confidential Club materials, records, and communication channels.

9. Submit feedback, ideas, or concerns openly or anonymously without fear of retaliation.

10. Request clarification on Executive Committee decisions and appeal disciplinary actions fairly (Article‚ÄØ6.3; DICSP Article‚ÄØ6.3).

11. Be afforded equal respect regardless of academic year, background, or Club role.

12. Utilize anonymous grievance reporting mechanisms provided by the Club and DICSP.

13. Initiate new Club-related projects, propose activities, and run for office in accordance with Club and DICSP election policies.

14. Access transparent information on Club budgets, performance reports, and governance records.

15. Be given fair hearings and respectful representation in all Club matters.

### **5.4 Member Benefits**

Members shall be entitled to:

1. Access to exclusive Club-organized events, workshops, and training sessions.

2. Receipt of participation certificates and eligibility for recognition awards.

3. Issuance of a digital or physical Club ID, as provided by DICSP.

4. Opportunities for leadership development, skill enhancement, and training through Club roles and activities.

### **5.5 Minimum Involvement**

1. Ordinary and Associate Members are expected to attend at least **two general meetings per academic term**.

2. Ordinary and Associate Members are expected to participate in at least **one major Club activity or event per academic semester**.

### **5.6 Register of Members**

The Executive Committee shall appoint a dedicated officer responsible for maintaining an accurate and up-to-date register of all Club members. This register shall be available for inspection by any member at reasonable times, in compliance with DICSP data privacy guidelines.

## **Article 6: Termination of Membership**

### **6.1 Grounds for Termination**

Membership in the DIC Programming Club (DICPC) may be terminated under any of the following circumstances:

1. Violation of the Code of Conduct as detailed in Article‚ÄØ12.

2. Formal resignation submitted by the member in writing.

3. Expulsion initiated by the Executive Committee following due process.

4. Failure to pay the annual membership fee within two (2) months of the due date.

5. Demonstrated inactivity for one (1) full academic term, as determined by the Executive Committee in accordance with Article‚ÄØ5.5.

6. Misconduct, disrespect toward other members or leadership, or breach of stated Club values.

7. Falsification of information provided in the membership application or failure to fulfill assigned duties and responsibilities.

### **6.2 Executive Committee Authority**

The Executive Committee shall have the authority to suspend or expel any member for:

1. Any grounds listed in Article‚ÄØ6.1.

2. Submission of false or inaccurate statements in the membership application.

3. Breach of any Club rule, regulation, or by-law.

4. Any act deemed detrimental to the Club or Daffodil International College, following a due inquiry process.

### **6.3 Appeals Process**

1. Any member whose membership is suspended, terminated, or revoked has the right to appeal in writing to the Executive Committee within seven (7) working days of notification.

2. An appeal hearing shall be convened at a General Meeting called specifically for this purpose or at a Special Meeting chaired by the Conflict Resolution Officer in consultation with the Faculty Advisor.

3. The decision rendered at the appeal hearing shall be final, in accordance with DICSP grievance and escalation procedures (DICSP Article‚ÄØ6.3).

## **Article 7: Executive Committee**

### **7.0 Composition and Election**

1\)  The management of the Club shall be vested in the Executive Committee, elected by financial members at the Annual General Meeting.

2\) The Executive Committee shall consist of the following Office Bearers:

* President  
* Vice President  
* Secretary  
* Treasurer  
* Technical Lead (Competitive Programming)  
* Development Lead (Software/Web Development)  
* Event Coordinator  
* Content & Publication Lead  
* Media & Design Lead  
* Outreach Coordinator  
* Training Coordinator  
* Conflict Resolution Officer

1. Executive positions shall be held for one (1) academic year and are subject to annual elections. No individual may concurrently hold more than one executive position (DICSP Article‚ÄØ10).

2. All positions shall be eligible for re-election at the Annual General Meeting, provided the member meets eligibility criteria.

### **7.1 Quorum**

A quorum for Executive Committee meetings shall consist of **half of the currently serving Executive Committee members plus one**.

### **7.2 Chairperson**

In the absence of the President or Vice President, a chairperson shall be nominated by the attending Executive Committee members to preside over the meeting.

### **7.3 Loss of Seat**

An Executive Committee member may forfeit their position for any of the following reasons:

1. Absence from **three (3) or more Executive Committee meetings** within an academic semester without prior approval.

2. Failure to maintain financial membership in the Club.

3. Violation of the Code of Conduct (Article‚ÄØ12) resulting in disciplinary action.

## **Article 8: Powers of the Executive Committee**

The Executive Committee shall be responsible for the day-to-day administration and governance of the DIC Programming Club (DICPC) and shall exercise the following powers:

1. **Planning and Oversight:** Plan, approve, and oversee all Club events, activities, and initiatives in accordance with DICSP event management guidelines (DICSP Article‚ÄØ7).

3. **Membership Oversight:** Review membership applications, renewals, and enforce membership policies as outlined in Articles‚ÄØ5 and‚ÄØ6.

5. **Subcommittees:** Establish or disband subcommittees as required to achieve specific Club objectives.

7. **Banking and Finance Administration:** Administer the Club‚Äôs finances, appoint authorized signatories, direct the opening and closing of banking accounts, and transfer funds between accounts, provided all transactions are approved by the Executive Committee and comply with DICSP financial regulations.

9. **Internal Adjudication:** Adjudicate on all internal Club matters affecting operations, reputation, or member well-being.

13. **Filling Vacancies:** Appoint a successor to any vacancy on the Executive Committee during the academic year until the next Annual General Meeting.

14. **Custody of Records:** Appoint officers or agents to have custody of the Club‚Äôs records, documents, and securities, ensuring their secure management in accordance with DICSP digital ethics and data privacy policies.

## **Article 9: General Meetings**

### **9.1 Annual General Meeting (AGM)**

1. The AGM shall be held before the conclusion of the academic calendar year.

2. The Secretary shall provide at least fourteen (14) days‚Äô written notice of the AGM to all financial members.

3. All financial members are encouraged to attend the AGM.

4. **Quorum:** The quorum for the AGM shall be 60% of the Executive Committee members and ten (10) general members. If the quorum is not met within thirty (30) minutes of the scheduled start, the meeting shall be adjourned for one week to the same time and place. If the quorum is still not met at the adjourned meeting, those present, including a minimum of the Executive Committee, shall constitute a quorum.

5. **Agenda:** The AGM shall consider the following business:

   * Opening of the meeting

   * Apologies

   * Confirmation of the minutes of the previous AGM

   * Presentation and adoption of the Annual Report

   * Presentation of the Treasurer‚Äôs financial statement

   * Election of new Executive Committee members

   * Vote of thanks to outgoing Executive members

   * Determination of annual membership fees

   * Notices of motion

   * Any urgent business (at the discretion of the presiding officer)

   * Closure

### **9.2 General Meetings**

1. General Meetings may be convened by the Executive Committee, the President and Secretary, or upon the written request of at least ten percent (10%) of financial members.

2. The Secretary shall provide at least seven (7) days‚Äô written notice to all financial members.

3. Notices shall specify the business for which the meeting has been called; no other business shall be conducted.

4. **Quorum:** The quorum shall be 60% of the Executive Committee members and ten (10) general members. If the quorum is not met, the meeting shall be adjourned for one week to the same time and place. If still unmet, a minimum of the Executive Committee members present shall constitute the quorum for the conduct of the specified business.

## **Article 10: Voting and Elections**

### **10.1 Voting at Annual General Meetings and General Meetings**

1. The President shall have a deliberative vote and, in the event of a tied vote, shall exercise a casting vote.

2. Each financial member present shall have one (1) vote.

3. Elections for Executive Committee positions shall be conducted transparently and supervised by the DICSP and the Club‚Äôs Faculty Advisor, in accordance with DICSP election protocols (DICSP Article‚ÄØ5).

4. Nominations for Executive Committee positions must be submitted no less than five (5) days prior to the scheduled election date.

   ### **10.2 Voting at Executive Committee Meetings**

5. The President shall have a deliberative vote and, in the event of a tied vote, shall exercise a casting vote.

6. Each Executive Committee member present shall have one (1) vote.

## **Article 11: Finance**

1. All funds of the Club shall be deposited into DICSP-approved accounts at such banks or recognized financial institutions as determined by the Executive Committee in consultation with the DICSP Finance & Sponsorship Committee.

2. Payments due by the Club shall be made by cheque, direct debit, or cash, provided that such payments have been formally approved at an Executive Committee Meeting. When immediate payment is necessary, the payment may be made, subject to endorsement at the next Executive Committee Meeting. Disbursements shall require two authorized signatures: the Treasurer and one other designated Executive Committee member.

3. The Secretary shall not expend more than the approved petty cash limit without prior written consent of the Executive Committee and shall maintain a detailed record of all such expenditures in a Petty Cash Book.

4. The Treasurer shall present a comprehensive statement of the Club‚Äôs financial position at each Executive Committee Meeting.

5. A detailed statement of Income and Expenditure, and of Assets and Liabilities, shall be submitted to the Annual General Meeting. The report of the appointed auditor, approved by DICSP, shall be attached to the financial report.

6. The Club‚Äôs financial year shall commence on January‚ÄØ1 of each year. All accounts, books, and financial records of the Club shall be audited annually.

7. All external sponsorships require prior approval from the DICSP Finance & Sponsorship Committee and must comply with DICSP sponsorship policies (DICSP Article‚ÄØ8).

8. The Club‚Äôs budget must align with planned activities and shall be submitted to DICSP as part of quarterly and annual reporting.

9. Signatories to the Club‚Äôs accounts shall include the Treasurer and one (1) of the following: President, Vice President, or Secretary.

10. All property and income of the Club shall be applied solely toward the promotion of the Club‚Äôs objectives. No part of the property or income shall be distributed, directly or indirectly, to members, except in good faith for the promotion of these objectives (e.g., prizes for events or reimbursement for Club-related expenses).

## **Article 12: Code of Conduct**

All members and executive officers of the DIC Programming Club (DICPC) are expected to uphold the highest standards of conduct, integrity, and professionalism, consistent with the overarching Code of Conduct of the Daffodil International College Students‚Äô Panel (DICSP Article‚ÄØ10).

### **12.1 Expected Conduct**

All members shall:

1. Uphold and promote respect, equity, and collaboration within the Club and the wider college community.

2. Maintain respectful, humble, and professional behavior toward all peers, faculty, staff, and visitors, irrespective of seniority or position.

3. Be punctual, dependable, and conscientious in fulfilling all assigned responsibilities and duties.

4. Promote teamwork, mutual support, and constructive engagement among all Club members.

5. Maintain integrity, honesty, and transparency in all Club operations, including financial dealings and reporting.

6. Adhere to and promote the values, policies, and regulations of Daffodil International College and DICSP in all actions and representations.

7. Use Club branding, digital platforms, and official communication channels responsibly and in compliance with DICSP branding guidelines.

8. Attend all official meetings, providing prior notice to the Secretary in case of absence with valid justification.

9. Obtain prior approval from the Executive Committee if participating in multiple clubs to avoid conflicts of interest.

10. Complete assigned tasks within deadlines and report promptly any issues or challenges encountered.

### **12.2 Prohibited Conduct**

Members shall not:

1. Engage in any form of misconduct, harassment, discrimination, or bullying, whether verbal, physical, or digital.

2. Participate in political, abusive, or religiously sensitive activities within the Club, at Club-sponsored events, or in any capacity representing the Club.

3. Share confidential or personal information of members, officers, or the Club without explicit, prior consent, in accordance with DICSP data privacy policies (DICSP Article‚ÄØ11).

4. Misuse Club assets, funds, intellectual property, branding, or reputation for personal gain or unauthorized purposes.

5. Disrupt meetings, events, or Club operations through inappropriate behavior or interference.

6. Undermine, disregard, or act contrary to the decisions, policies, or directives of the Executive Committee or DICSP.

7. Communicate in an aggressive, abusive, disrespectful, or otherwise unprofessional manner toward any member, officer, faculty, or staff.

8. Hold more than one primary executive position in any student club concurrently (DICSP Article‚ÄØ10).

9. Misuse Club digital platforms, online accounts, or social media channels in a manner that could harm the Club‚Äôs reputation, violate privacy, or compromise confidential information.

10. Engage in any activity, conduct, or behavior that contravenes applicable laws, College regulations, or DICSP policies.

**Enforcement:** Violations of this Article may result in disciplinary action, including suspension or termination of membership, in accordance with Articles‚ÄØ6 and‚ÄØ12 of this Constitution.

## **Article 13: Amendments and Policy Updates**

### **13.1 Alteration, Repeal, or Addition**

No alteration, repeal, or addition to this Constitution shall be made except at the Annual General Meeting or at a General Meeting expressly convened for that purpose.

### **13.2 Notice of Motion**

Written notice of all motions to alter, repeal, or add to the Constitution shall be furnished to all financial members in advance: fourteen (14) days prior to the Annual General Meeting, or seven (7) days prior to a General Meeting called for that purpose.

### **13.3 Transmission of Notices**

The Secretary shall ensure that each member of the Executive Committee receives the text of all notices of motion not less than fourteen (14) days before the Annual General Meeting, or seven (7) days before the relevant General Meeting.

### **13.4 By‚ÄëLaws Alteration Procedure**

Alterations to the By‚Äëlaws of the Club may be made only at a meeting of the Executive Committee, provided that the proposed alteration(s) have been circulated in writing to all Committee members not less than seven (7) days prior to the meeting.

### **13.5 Voting Thresholds**

1. A motion to alter, repeal, or add to this Constitution shall have no effect unless passed by a ‚Äúspecial resolution,‚Äù defined as a seventy-five percent (75%) majority of those present and entitled to vote at the relevant meeting.

2. An amendment may be proposed by a minimum of three (3) Executive Committee members, requiring a two-thirds (2/3) majority at a formally convened Executive Committee meeting for approval. Final ratification of any amendment shall be subject to approval by the DICSP Executive Council and the Faculty Advisory Panel.

### **13.6 Emergency Amendments**

In exceptional circumstances where urgent amendment(s) to this Constitution or its By‚Äëlaws are deemed necessary, the Executive Committee may seek written approval from the DICSP Executive Council for such amendment(s) to take immediate effect. Such amendment(s) shall be subject to full ratification at the next duly convened General Meeting of the Club.

### **13.7 Annual Review**

This Constitution shall be reviewed annually from January to March, aligning with the DICSP review cycle. Any member or the Faculty Advisor may submit suggestions for amendment during the review period.

## **Article 14: Dissolution**

In the event of the dissolution of the DIC Programming Club (DICPC), any property or assets remaining after the satisfaction of all debts, liabilities, and costs of winding up shall be distributed as follows:

1. To another incorporated club with objectives similar to those of DICPC, as determined by resolution of the members in consultation with DICSP; or

2. For charitable purposes approved by DICSP, with the specific club or charitable purpose determined by resolution of the members.

All remaining assets and documentation shall be formally returned to DICSP for archival purposes and appropriate redistribution. Any remaining funds shall be redirected either to a similar club recognized by DICSP or held by DICSP specifically for general educational use within Daffodil International College.

## **Article 15: Member Data Privacy and Security**

### **15.1 Purpose**

This Article establishes the framework for the collection, use, protection, and disclosure of personal information of members of the DIC Programming Club (DICPC), in accordance with DICSP policies and best practices in data privacy.

### **15.2 Data Collection**

The Club shall collect only information necessary for the administration of Club activities, including:

* Full Name and Student ID

* Contact Information: Email and Phone Number

* Academic Information: Year of study, GPA, attendance records, participation in activities, and membership fee status

### **15.3 Purpose of Data Use**

Member information shall be used solely for:

1. Maintaining the official Register of Members

2. Communication regarding Club meetings, events, and activities

3. Verifying eligibility for membership, executive roles, and participation in competitions and events

4. Reporting and auditing as required by Daffodil International College

### **15.4 Data Protection and Confidentiality**

1. Access to member information is restricted to Executive Committee members performing official duties.

2. All records shall be stored on secure, password-protected systems.

3. Suspected data breaches must be reported immediately to the Faculty Advisor and affected members.

4. Executive Committee members are obligated to maintain strict confidentiality of all member information.

### **15.5 Data Sharing and Consent**

1. Member data shall not be sold, traded, or disclosed to any external party.

2. Internal disclosure is permitted only for official purposes, including audits, disciplinary reviews, and event approvals.

3. Sharing sensitive personal information with any party requires the explicit consent of the member.

### **15.6 Member Rights**

1. Members may request access to their personal information and request corrections where necessary.

2. Members may withdraw consent for non-essential data processing, except where required for mandatory record-keeping purposes.

3. Members may report concerns regarding the handling or misuse of their data to the Executive Committee or Faculty Advisor.

### **15.7 Enforcement**

Any violation of this Article shall be treated as a breach of the DICPC Constitution and may result in disciplinary action in accordance with Articles‚ÄØ6 and‚ÄØ12.
